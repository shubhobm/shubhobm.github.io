About Me

 

I am a senior year Ph.D. student in the School of Statistics of the University of Minnesota, my broad area of focus is statistical model selection, dimension reduction and robust statistics. My thesis advisor is Prof. Anshu Chatterjee.

I am passionate about substantial applications of cutting-edge statistical methodologies in real life problems; and am involved in several projects dealing with high-dimensional (n << p) and big-data problems in human genetics, statistical chemistry and climate science.
